Thomas Joseph Mboya

Founding father of the republic of Kenya

A timeline of Tom Mboya's life

  • 1930 - Born on 15th August in Kilima Mbogo near Thika town.
  • 1942 - Joined a Catholic Secondary School in Yala, Nyanza Province. St. Mary's Yala.
  • 1946 - Went to Holy Ghost College(later Mangu highchool) passed well enough to do his Cambridge School Certificate.
  • 1948 - Joined the royal Sanitary Institute's Medical Training School for sanitary inspectors at Nairobi.
  • 1950 - Worked as a sanitary inspector in Nairobi.
  • 1955 - Attended Ruskin college, Oxford, where he studied Industrial Management.
  • 1956 - Graduated and returned to Kenya and joined politics at the start of the MAUMAU uprising.
  • 1957 - Formed his own party, The People's Congress party.
  • 1958 - Elected chairman during the All African Peoples' Conference in Ghana, convened by Kwame Nkrumah.
  • 1959 - Together with the African-American students Foundation in the United States, Mboya organized the Airlift Africa Project, through which 81 Kenyan Students were flown to the U.S. to study at U.S universities. Barrack Obama,Sr. received a scholarship through this foundation
  • 1960 - Kennedy Foundation agreed to underwrite the airlift after Mboya visited Senator Jack Kennedy to ask for assistance. Airlift Africa was extended to Uganda, Tanganyika & Zanzibar (now Tanzania), Nothern Rhodesia (now Zambia), Southern Rhodesia(now Zimbabwe), and Nyasaland(now Malawi). 230 African students received scholarships to study in the United States due to this. Hundreds more between 1961-1963
  • 1960 - Merged his party with Kenya African Union & Kenya Independence Movement to form the Kenya African National Union which would later become Kenya's ruling Party after independence in 1963.
  • 1963 - After independence on 1st June 1963, Mboya was elected as an MP for Nairobi Central Constituency & become minister of Justice and constitutional affairs and later Minister for Economic planning and Development. In this role he wrote the important "Sessional Paper 10" on harambee and the Principles of African Socialism. A model of Government based on African values.
  • 1969 - Assassinated on the 5th of July. Aged just 38. His current station then was Minister for Economic Planning and Development.

"Tom Mboya's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and vision can make to human peace and progress."
